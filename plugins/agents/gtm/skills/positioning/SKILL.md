Define positioning for $ARGUMENTS using the April Dunford framework.

## Process

1. **Competitive alternatives** — What would customers do if your product didn't exist? (not just direct competitors — include manual processes, spreadsheets, hiring someone)
2. **Unique attributes** — What do you have that the alternatives don't? (features, capabilities, approach)
3. **Value** — What does each unique attribute enable for the customer? (map attribute → benefit)
4. **Target customer** — Who cares most about this value? (the segment that finds your unique attributes most valuable)
5. **Market category** — What frame of reference makes your value obvious? (the category that makes your positioning intuitive)

## Output

Present as a positioning canvas, then draft a one-paragraph positioning statement and a one-sentence tagline.
