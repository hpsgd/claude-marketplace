# AI Governance Review: Candidate Screener (Workbench HR Platform)

## Classification

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Use case** | AI-assisted candidate ranking — GPT-4o reads job applications and assigns a 1–5 score shown to hiring managers to prioritise review queue |
| **Risk level** | **HIGH** |
| **Review date** | 2026-04-30 |
| **Reviewer** | GRC Lead |

**Reasoning:** Hiring and recruitment decisions affecting individual candidates are a textbook High-risk use case under both the EU AI Act (Annex III, §4 — employment, workers management, and access to self-employment) and NIST AI RMF (high-stakes individual-impact domain). A human hiring manager makes the final call, but the blast radius is wide: every candidate's application ordering is determined by the model, and anchoring bias means candidates ranked lower are systematically less likely to receive attention regardless of actual merit. The use of demographic-adjacent data (age, location, name) elevates this further. Classification cannot be reduced to Medium on the basis that a human approves: the human review loop does not neutralise the ordering effect. When uncertain, classify one level higher.

## Deployment Decision

**Decision: BLOCKED**

**Blocking gaps preventing deployment:**

1. No bias evaluation across demographic groups — age, location, and name are in-scope demographic-adjacent inputs with known bias vectors. No evidence of fairness testing across candidate cohorts.
2. Age and location fields should not enter the prompt at all — these are quasi-protected attributes. Data minimisation required before deployment.
3. No documented GDPR/Privacy Act lawful basis for processing candidate PII via a third-party AI provider (OpenAI). Article 22 automated decisioning implications not addressed.
4. No fallback for GPT-4o unavailability — a single provider with no documented fallback means the feature is either unavailable or proceeds without ranking (uncontrolled degradation).
5. No evidence of prompt version control in the repository.

These gaps are each independently blocking. The feature must not be deployed until all five are resolved.
