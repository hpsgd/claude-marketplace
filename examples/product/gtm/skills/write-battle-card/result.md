## TL;DR

We win when the buyer needs exec-grade reporting, cross-team rollup, and configurable workflows without heavy IT involvement. We lose when the buyer prioritises UI polish for first-time users, breadth of native integrations, or brand recognition. ## Where we lose (be honest)

1. **UI polish for first-time users** — Monday's kanban interface is more familiar to non-technical buyers who have seen it in previous roles. Clearpath's interface is more powerful but has a steeper first impression. Mitigation: ask for a live demo with real data rather than a trial with empty boards — Clearpath's advantage shows under real conditions.
