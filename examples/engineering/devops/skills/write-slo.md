# SLO Definition: PaymentGateway

## Service Profile

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Service name** | PaymentGateway |
| **What it does** | Processes card payment authorisations and captures for the checkout flow |
| **Users** | End customers completing purchases; internal order management system |
| **User-facing endpoints** | `POST /v1/charges` (authorise), `POST /v1/charges/{id}/capture` (capture), `GET /v1/charges/{id}` (status) |
| **What "down" means to users** | Customer cannot complete a purchase; card charge fails or times out; duplicate charge occurs |
| **Dependencies** | Stripe API (card processing), PostgreSQL (transaction records), Redis (idempotency cache) |
| **Current reliability** | 99.6% availability over last 30 days; p99 latency 380ms; error rate 0.4% |

Note: a 30-second Stripe API failover that is invisible to the calling service is not "down" for our users. A 500ms latency spike that causes the checkout page to time out IS "down" — from the customer's perspective the payment failed.

## Service Level Indicators

| SLI name | Category | What it measures | Good event definition | Bad event definition | Measurement method |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Availability** | Availability | Can customers complete a payment? | `POST /v1/charges` returns 200 or 402 (card declined — expected) | Returns 5xx or connection timeout | Load balancer access logs — ratio of non-5xx to total requests |
| **Latency** | Latency | Is authorisation fast enough for checkout? | Request completes in < 500ms | Request takes >= 500ms | Server-side histogram at p50, p95, p99 |
| **Correctness** | Quality | Does a captured charge actually settle? | Charge captured in our DB matches Stripe settlement within 24h | Discrepancy between captured and settled amount | Nightly reconciliation job |
| **Idempotency** | Quality | Do retried requests avoid duplicate charges? | Retry with same idempotency key returns same result, no duplicate charge | Duplicate charge created for same idempotency key | Stripe webhook events vs internal records |

Note: CPU utilisation and database connection pool usage are not SLIs. They are implementation details. A database at 90% CPU that still serves requests within 500ms is meeting the latency SLI.

## Service Level Objectives

| SLI | SLO target | Measurement window | Error budget | Error budget in time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Availability | 99.7% | Rolling 30 days | 0.3% = ~130 min/month | 130 minutes of failed requests/month |
| Latency (p99) | 98% of requests < 500ms | Rolling 30 days | 2% can exceed 500ms | ~864 min of slow requests/month |
| Correctness | 99.99% | Rolling 30 days | 0.01% | ~4.3 min equivalent of incorrect settlements/month |

| SLO | Downtime per year | Downtime per month | Downtime per week |
|---|---|---|---|
| 99% | 3.65 days | 7.3 hours | 1.68 hours |
| 99.5% | 1.83 days | 3.65 hours | 50.4 min |
| 99.9% | 8.77 hours | 43.8 min | 10.1 min |

**Target rationale:** Current reliability is 99.6%. Setting 99.7% puts the target slightly above current performance but within reach. Setting 99.99% would be aspirational fiction — we'd burn through that budget in under a week at current rates. Revisit after 90 days of data.

## Error Budget Policy

**Error budget owner:** Alex Chen (Head of Payments Engineering — single person accountable)

### When error budget is healthy (> 50% remaining)

- Normal development velocity
- Deploy at standard frequency (up to twice daily)
- Feature work proceeds as planned

### When error budget is depleting (25–50% remaining)

- Review recent deployments for reliability regressions
- Increase deployment testing rigour — require integration test sign-off
- No high-risk changes (new payment providers, schema migrations) without rollback plan

### When error budget is critical (< 25% remaining)

- Freeze non-critical feature deployments
- Prioritise reliability work: bug fixes, capacity, redundancy
- Incident review for all recent budget-consuming events
- Daily error budget check with Alex Chen and engineering lead

### When error budget is exhausted (0% remaining)

- **Feature freeze** — only reliability improvements and critical security fixes deploy
- Mandatory post-mortem for the incident(s) that consumed the budget
- Alex Chen and product owner agree on reliability investment before features resume
- Budget must recover to > 25% before feature freeze lifts

### Error budget exceptions

- Planned maintenance windows (e.g. DB failover test) deducted from a separate maintenance budget
- Stripe API outages counted in the error budget but flagged separately in monthly review — these inform Stripe SLA negotiation, not internal engineering priorities

## Alerting

### Fast burn alerts (page — immediate response required)

| Alert | Condition | Window | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Availability critical** | Burn rate > 14.4x (exhausts 30d budget in 50h) | 5 min over 1 hour | Page on-call, begin incident response |
| **Latency critical** | p99 latency burn rate > 14.4x | 5 min over 1 hour | Page on-call, investigate |

### Slow burn alerts (ticket — investigate within business hours)

| Alert | Condition | Window | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Availability degraded** | Burn rate > 1x sustained | 6 hours over 3 days | Create ticket, investigate |
| **Latency degraded** | p99 > 500ms sustained | 6 hours over 3 days | Create ticket, review capacity |
| **Budget warning** | Error budget < 50% remaining | Rolling 30 days | Notify Alex Chen |
| **Budget critical** | Error budget < 25% remaining | Rolling 30 days | Trigger error budget policy escalation |

### Burn rate calculation

- **Fast burn:** 14.4x = consuming 30-day budget in ~50 hours (appropriate for a payment service where 2 hours of degradation is significant)
- **Slow burn:** 1x sustained = on track to exhaust budget by end of window
- Alert on burn rate, not raw error counts. A 10-minute spike that consumes 0.05% of budget does not need a page. A sustained 0.4% error rate burning 4x normal budget rate does.

## Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Attendees | Agenda |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Error budget check** | Weekly (Monday) | Alex Chen, on-call engineer | Budget remaining, burn rate trend, upcoming risk changes |
| **SLO review** | Monthly | Alex Chen, engineering lead, product owner | SLO appropriateness, budget policy compliance, incidents |
| **SLO recalibration** | Quarterly | Engineering lead, product owner, stakeholders | Tighten/relax targets based on data, user feedback |

### Criteria for tightening SLOs

- Error budget consistently unspent (> 80% remaining at end of window for 3+ months)
- User complaints about payment reliability despite SLO being met — current targets are too loose
- Dependency SLOs (Stripe) have tightened, allowing tighter internal targets

### Criteria for relaxing SLOs

- Error budget consistently exhausted despite reasonable engineering investment (not just incidents)
- Current SLO is tighter than checkout conversion data supports — users don't notice sub-600ms latency
- Cost of meeting the latency SLO is disproportionate to business value
