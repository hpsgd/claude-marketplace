### What Has Been Legislated — Mandatory Requirements in Force or Confirmed

**EU — CSRD / ESRS (Mandatory, phased, recently narrowed)**

Wave 1 companies (formerly under NFRD) began mandatory reporting against European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) for FY2024. However, the **Omnibus I directive** (adopted February 2026) substantially narrowed scope — the revised regime now applies only to companies with **>1,000 employees and >€450M turnover**. Wave 2 and 3 companies had obligations postponed via a "stop-the-clock" mechanism (April 2025). Simplified ESRS must be adopted within six months of Omnibus entry into force.

Notably, CSRD captures large **private** companies too, not just listed ones — broader than any other jurisdiction surveyed.

**UK — TCFD-aligned rules (Mandatory, transitioning)**

Mandatory TCFD-aligned disclosure has applied to premium listed companies since **1 January 2022**, extended to standard listed companies from **1 January 2023**. The FCA launched consultation CP26/5 (January 2026) proposing to replace these with ISSB-aligned UK Sustainability Reporting Standards (UK SRS), with an effective date of **1 January 2027**. Scope 3 emissions proposed as "comply or explain" from 2028.

**Australia — AASB S2 under Corporations Act (Mandatory, commenced)**

Australia has the most operationally advanced mandatory regime outside the EU. AASB S2 (closely modelled on IFRS S2/ISSB) commenced for **Group 1** entities from **1 January 2025**, phased across three groups:

| Group | Threshold | Start date |
|---|---|---|
| Group 1 | Revenue >$500M, assets >$250M, or emissions >100kt CO2-e | 1 Jan 2025 |
| Group 2 | Revenue $200–500M, assets $100–250M, or 25–100kt emissions | 1 Jul 2026 |
| Group 3 | Revenue $25–200M, assets $12.5–100M, or lower thresholds | 1 Jul 2027 |

Scope 1, 2, and 3 required from the outset. AASB S1 (general sustainability) is voluntary; only AASB S2 (climate) is mandatory.

**Japan — SSBJ Standards (Mandatory pathway confirmed, effective 2027–2029)**

A general sustainability materiality obligation has applied to listed companies since **January 2023** under the amended Cabinet Office Order. Full mandatory SSBJ standards (ISSB-aligned, issued March 2025) will apply to **Prime Market-listed companies** on a market-cap-tiered schedule:

| Market cap | Mandatory from |
|---|---|
| ≥ JPY 3 trillion | FY ending 31 March 2027 |
| ≥ JPY 1 trillion | FY ending 31 March 2028 |
| ≥ JPY 500 billion | FY ending 31 March 2029 |
| < JPY 500 billion | Under FSA consideration |

Independent assurance becomes mandatory from March 2028, phased by market cap.

### What Remains Voluntary

**US — SEC climate rules effectively suspended**

The SEC adopted final climate disclosure rules in March 2024, but they were **immediately stayed pending litigation**. In March 2025, the SEC voted to **withdraw its defense** of the rules. As of late 2025, the rules exist on paper but cannot be enforced and their fate is undetermined. No mandatory federal climate disclosure obligation exists in practice. Existing Regulation S-K materiality obligations technically require disclosure of material climate risks, but there is no standalone mandatory climate framework.

**Canada — Mandatory rule paused indefinitely**

The Canadian Securities Administrators **paused development of NI 51-107** in April 2025, citing the SEC's withdrawal and competitiveness concerns. The CSSB published voluntary standards CSDS 1 and CSDS 2 (ISSB-aligned) in December 2024, effective voluntarily from 1 January 2025. Scope 3 deferred for voluntary adopters to 2027. No mandatory adoption date exists.

### Key Variations Across Jurisdictions

**Who must report**

The EU is the broadest — all large companies (listed or private) meeting the employee/revenue thresholds. Australia covers listed companies, large private companies, and certain financial entities by size. UK and Japan requirements are primarily for listed companies. Canada and the US have no confirmed mandatory scope.

**Base standard — ESRS vs ISSB**

A structural bifurcation has emerged:

| Jurisdiction | Standard | Relationship to ISSB |
|---|---|---|
| EU | ESRS (EFRAG) | Partial alignment; **double materiality** (financial + impact) differs from ISSB's financial materiality |
| UK | UK SRS (proposed) | Direct ISSB adoption with UK modifications |
| Australia | AASB S2 | ISSB-aligned with AU modifications |
| Japan | SSBJ Standards | ISSB-aligned |
| Canada | CSDS 2 (voluntary) | ISSB-aligned |
| US | SEC rules (stayed) | Not ISSB-based; domestic SEC framework |

The EU's **double materiality** requirement — disclosing both financial impact *on* the company AND the company's impact on society/environment — is a structural non-equivalence with ISSB-adopting jurisdictions.

**Scope 3 treatment**

The most contested element:

- **Australia**: Required from the start of mandatory reporting
- **EU (ESRS)**: Required, with a value chain cap limiting requests to smaller suppliers
- **Japan (SSBJ)**: Included in mandatory standards
- **UK**: Proposed as "comply or explain" from 2028
- **Canada**: Deferred to 2027 for voluntary adopters
- **US**: Would have applied only to large accelerated filers when material or when included in a GHG target

### Key Uncertainties

1. **EU simplified ESRS**: The delegated act for the revised Omnibus standards has not been adopted yet — unclear exactly which data points will remain mandatory under the narrowed regime.
2. **US trajectory**: SEC climate rules are neither enforced nor formally dead. The Eighth Circuit placed litigation in abeyance (September 2025), directing the SEC to decide their fate. California SB 253/SB 261 partially fill the gap but face ongoing legal challenge.
3. **UK SRS finalisation**: CP26/5 consultation is open as of this research; the proposed 2027 start date and Scope 3 treatment remain subject to change.
4. **Canada re-engagement**: Pause declared with no resumption timeline. Explicitly tied to US regulatory developments — if the US position shifts, Canada may reconsider.
5. **Japan sub-JPY500B cohort**: FSA has not decided whether smaller Prime Market companies face mandatory requirements.
6. **ESRS–ISSB interoperability**: Double vs. financial materiality creates a structural non-equivalence. Multinationals reporting across both regimes face dual reporting burdens despite EFRAG/ISSB interoperability guidance.
