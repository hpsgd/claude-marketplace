### Differentiation analysis

**On price:** Cloud Payroll and KeyPay win for price-sensitive buyers. At ~$3–4/employee/month, they undercut Employment Hero by 50–60% for pure payroll. For buyers who want payroll only and handle HR elsewhere, this tier wins. Xero and MYOB compete on bundle economics, not unit pricing — they win when the accounting relationship already exists.

**On AU compliance depth:** KeyPay wins this dimension outright. Its award interpretation engine handles modern award complexity better than any competitor, which matters most for hospitality, retail, and trades — segments with the highest award complexity. Employment Hero is competent on STP2 and Fair Work but loses on edge-case award scenarios.

**On feature breadth (HRIS + payroll):** Employment Hero wins, and it is not close. It is the only AU-native player with a full HR suite (onboarding, performance, benefits, payroll) under one roof at SMB price points. The Flare HR acquisition accelerated this. For a buyer who wants to reduce the number of HR tools, Employment Hero is the default recommendation.

**On accountant channel reach:** Xero Payroll and KeyPay split this market. Xero wins with bookkeeping-led practices; KeyPay wins with payroll bureau practices. MYOB holds a legacy position here but is losing ground. A new entrant targeting accountant-led distribution faces a duopoly.

**On scheduling and time-attendance:** Deputy wins with no close second for businesses that run shift-based rosters. The Deputy Pay launch (Mar 2026) now means Deputy is entering the payroll market from the scheduling side — a structurally interesting move that Employment Hero should watch.

**White space note:** The 20–100 employee segment with complex leave workflows (multi-award, casual + part-time mix) and no dedicated HR person is currently underserved. Employment Hero is too feature-heavy and expensive for this buyer; Cloud Payroll is too thin. KeyPay covers the payroll side well but has no native leave request/approval workflow. A product positioning around "compliant AU payroll with modern leave management, no HRIS overhead, for the businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets but don't need a full HR platform" has no direct incumbent in this pocket. For a funding pitch, this is the defensible wedge.
