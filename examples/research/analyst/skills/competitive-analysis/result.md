### Differentiation analysis

**AU compliance (awards/Fair Work)**
foundU wins this axis for businesses with complex modern award obligations — particularly hospitality, retail, and labour hire. Its native award and EBA interpreter is purpose-built and tightly coupled to the payroll engine. Tanda is the closest comparable for shift-heavy environments. Employment Hero's HeroForce is a structural bet on compliance-as-a-service (co-employment), which is a different and potentially larger answer to the same problem. Xero and MYOB handle STP and basic award coverage adequately but route complexity to accountant-partners — they are not the right choice for a 50-person hospitality business managing multiple awards. Definitiv handles the most complex multi-award scenarios but is priced out of the 10–200 segment entirely.

**Ease of use**
Xero wins for businesses that prioritise frictionless onboarding, particularly where the buyer is already on Xero accounting. The June 2025 move to include payroll in all plans removes a configuration step that previously deterred smaller buyers. Employment Hero scores well on ease-of-use in reviews but carries a heavier implementation burden; its support model actively penalises buyers who get stuck. Microkeeper is underrated here — its entry tier is genuinely simple for sub-30-employee businesses that need STP payroll and leave tracking without the overhead of a full HRIS.

**Price**
At the bottom of the market (10–25 employees), Microkeeper is the price leader at ~$4/user/month on the Standard plan. Xero's Ignite plan is cheap for sole traders but scales poorly — at 25 employees, the $5/ee/month overage adds $125/month on top of the plan fee. foundU's $400/month minimum makes it expensive for the smallest buyers despite a competitive per-user rate. Employment Hero's $200/month minimum positions it firmly as a mid-SMB tool. MYOB AccountRight Plus at $165/month with unlimited payroll is strong value for established businesses already embedded in the MYOB accounting ecosystem.

**Integrations**
Xero wins decisively with 1,000+ marketplace integrations. Its dominance as the AU accounting platform means most adjacent tools (Deputy, Tanda, Microkeeper, foundU) integrate into it — which becomes a strategic moat. Employment Hero's closed-loop platform is an integration risk: it works best when it owns the full HR and payroll stack, and third-party connections are narrower. Rippling's integration strength (MDM, spend management, IT provisioning) is largely irrelevant to the typical AU SMB in the 10–200 range.

**Mid-market credibility (50–200 employees)**
Elmo Software is the most credible mid-market AU HR platform — deep feature breadth, AU/NZ compliance, and established enterprise reputation. Employment Hero is making the strongest push into this segment via HeroForce and KKR-backed growth: A$300M+ ARR and 300,000+ customers gives it the scale economics to move up-market. MYOB Business sits squarely in the 10–100 zone with its accounting heritage. Rippling has mid-market credibility globally but lacks proven AU award compliance depth to be a first choice without a verified local customer base.

**Market movement**
Three directional movements are decisive. First, **Xero is moving down-market**: bundling payroll into the $35/month Ignite tier is a land-and-expand move targeting the accountant channel and sole traders, pulling directly from MYOB at the small end. Second, **Employment Hero is moving up-market and laterally**: HeroForce, the KKR investment, and the Humi acquisition point toward an employment OS for 20–500 employees across multiple countries — not just an AU SMB payroll tool. Third, **Rippling is moving into AU from above**: the Sydney APAC HQ and stated commercial hiring plans signal a push into businesses that previously couldn't justify Rippling's implementation cost. It becomes relevant at ~50+ employees where its unified HR+IT+Finance story has a clear ROI — below that, AU compliance complexity and pricing create barriers.
