### Strategic Direction

- **AIP as the growth engine.** "Operationalised AI" via AIP Bootcamps is compressing enterprise AI deployments from months to days. US commercial AIP adoption is the primary growth driver.
- **Agentic AI positioning.** Foundry's "ontology" layer is being reframed as the governance/grounding layer for autonomous enterprise agents — counter-positioning to generic copilot tools.
- **Defence AI consolidation.** TITAN, Maven Smart System, Army $10B contract, ShipOS — Palantir is the Pentagon's primary AI software vendor. NATO and Five Eyes expansion follows.
- **International asymmetry.** US commercial +137% YoY in Q4 vs. international commercial +2%. No clear catalyst articulated for international commercial scale.
- **Ideology as commercial strategy.** Karp's book and manifesto are deliberate brand differentiation — positioning Palantir as the ideological vanguard of Western techno-defence capability. Commercial upside in US federal; reputational downside in Europe and ESG-sensitive contexts.
