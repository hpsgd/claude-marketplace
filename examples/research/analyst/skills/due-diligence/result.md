## Notes

The top gap is entity verification. The skill has no step for confirming ABN, legal entity name, or current registration status as a preflight check. For AU commercial partnerships this matters: the wrong legal entity could be the counterparty. ASIC Connect appears in the skill only under risk factors (Step 6), not as a scope-setting step. Adding a brief entity confirmation field to Step 1 (legal name, ABN, registered office, ASIC status) would close this gap cleanly.

The AU data-law gap is real but narrower. The skill routes correctly to ASIC for regulatory findings and checks OAIC for breach history, but does not prompt the analyst to assess whether customer-data sharing in the integration requires a Data Processing Agreement under the Privacy Act 1988, or whether SOCI Act obligations apply to either party's sector. For an AU-AU SaaS integration touching employee data, these are foreseeable questions. Currently they fall to analyst judgment rather than structured prompting.

The red-flag escalation table (Step 7) is the strongest structural feature of the skill — it prevents the analyst from stopping at a surface verdict when compounding signals are present, and it maps signal types to specific follow-on skills rather than leaving routing implicit.
