### Findings

#### Energy sector

The SOCI Act 2021 as amended designates electricity, gas, and liquid fuel assets as critical infrastructure. Electricity networks, generation assets >30MW, and gas transmission pipelines above specified thresholds are captured. (Source: [SOCI Act 2021 s.9](https://legislation.gov.au/Details/C2022C00138), accessed 2026-04-16; DISR sector brief — High confidence.)

The CIRMP obligation requires covered energy entities to develop, maintain, and annually review a risk management program addressing hazards defined in sector-specific rules. Energy sector rules were notified in August 2022 and became mandatory in August 2023. (Source: [Critical Infrastructure Rules 2023 (Energy Sector)](https://legislation.gov.au/Details/F2023L00976), accessed 2026-04-16 — High confidence.)

**Contested:** The threshold for "critical electricity asset" (>30MW) has been contested by smaller generation entities who argue the threshold is arbitrary and captures community-scale renewables projects not operationally critical to the grid. AEMO's submission to the 2023 post-implementation review noted the threshold was set pragmatically rather than risk-based. (Source: [AEMO submission to PJCIS 2023 review](https://aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Committees/Joint/Intelligence_and_Security/SoCIReview2023/Submissions), accessed 2026-04-16 — Medium confidence; position statement, not factual claim.) No resolution of this threshold question has been publicly announced.

#### Data storage sector

The "data storage or processing" sector was added in the 2022 amendments (SOCI Amendment (Critical Infrastructure Protection) Act 2022). The sector definition captures entities holding data on behalf of Government or from other critical sectors — but the precise scope of "data storage" versus cloud services versus managed IT services remains contested in practice. (Source: [Explanatory Memorandum to SOCI Amendment Act 2022](https://aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Bills_Legislation/Bills_Search_Results/Result?bId=r6959), accessed 2026-04-16 — High confidence; practical scope question is Contested/Unverified.)

The ACSC's published guidance for the data storage sector (released in phases from 2023) establishes a risk management framework but industry submissions — notably from AIIA and Australian DataCentres Association — indicate significant uncertainty about which entities are definitively captured as "responsible entities." (Source: [ACSC CIRMP Guidance — Data Storage Sector](https://cyber.gov.au/resources-business-and-government/essential-cyber-security/critical-infrastructure/data-storage), accessed 2026-04-16; [AIIA submission to PJCIS 2023](https://aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Committees/Joint/Intelligence_and_Security/SoCIReview2023/Submissions), accessed 2026-04-16 — Medium confidence for the uncertainty claim.)

### Contested findings

**1. Data storage sector scope**

*Position A (ACSC/Home Affairs):* The Act and rules define covered entities; if you hold data on behalf of a Government entity or critical infrastructure, you are covered. The framework is designed to be broad.

*Position B (industry — AIIA, DataCentres Association):* The definition in the Act is ambiguous for multi-tenant cloud and managed services providers. Many entities cannot determine from the statutory text whether they are a "responsible entity" without legal advice. ACSC guidance has not resolved the definitional ambiguity.

Evidence for Position A: Act text, ACSC guidance.
Evidence for Position B: AIIA submission (2023 PJCIS review), public comments from multiple datacentre operators, absence of a clear ACSC determination on the MSP/cloud question.

**2. Energy threshold**

*Position A (as enacted):* The 30MW threshold is the statutory definition. All covered entities know their obligations.

*Position B (industry):* The threshold is not risk-calibrated. It captures small community renewables with no national security relevance while potentially excluding some distributed assets above the threshold but below operational significance.

Evidence for Position A: Act text, DISR sector brief.
Evidence for Position B: AEMO submission (2023 PJCIS review).
