### Differentiation analysis

**AU compliance depth:** KeyPay and Employment Hero are winning here. Both have built AU-specific STP Phase 2, FairWork, and superannuation compliance natively. MYOB's compliance is strong but bundled to its accounting product. Rippling's compliance is improving but self-described as recent — G2 reviews confirm this is the most-cited gap vs incumbents.

**Price:** KeyPay wins on headline per-seat cost. Employment Hero wins when total cost of ownership includes the benefits marketplace (which reduces net cost for employers). Mid-market players (Elmo, Rippling) are higher price and not competing for the 10–200 seat segment directly.

**Integration breadth:** Rippling wins by a significant margin — its platform is explicitly designed around deep integrations with HRIS, IT, and finance systems. For SMBs that primarily need payroll and leave, this is over-engineered; for SMBs with existing tech stacks, it's a differentiator.

**Who is winning in the 10–200 seat AU payroll segment:** Employment Hero on brand and share, KeyPay on price, MYOB on accountant-channel distribution. Rippling is a future competitive threat rather than a current one at the SMB end of this segment.
